Successfully executed my Idea

I successfully implemented the Supply Chain Control Tower Project in Tata Steel’s Industrial By-Product Management Division. The brainchild of me and my team, it aimed at optimizing the supply chain inside the plant for steel scrap and wastes and reduce the cycle time of vehicles. I led the ground implementation of the project coordinating with IT and consulting teams to help establish the systems. The project is projected to save over 29 Cr. for the company making It my most successful project till date.

When I failed and learnt from Experience

In my initial days at Tata Steel, I was entrusted to be the second in the project on Plastic Recycling of wastes generated in plant and the areas under the Jamshedpur Utility and Services Company. Our idea was to manufacture plastic beads and sell them under the Tata brand name which would enhance the image of the company and push its sustainability cause. The project was innovative and there was scope but when we called a conference of bidders, hardly a couple were interested in taking this further and those who were later backed out as they believed their machines would be underutilized. Our analysis of their business model was weak and not as meticulous as it should have been. However, I learned so much about the plastic recycling process and its intricacies and the project also helped me build a rapport with my co-manager and other colleagues. It taught me that when things don’t pan out in spite of our best efforts, one should always stay motivated, look on the bright side and move on to new endeavors.

How initial chaos at my B-School changed me

These few months –my first encounter with VUCA were definitely not easy. Chaos was the new order. It was rigorous and grueling, but the experience was also life changing. Managing myself and my time were the biggest learnings I could derive out of it. But that’s the beauty of chaos. When your life is in chaos, you have no option but to fall in love with it.
